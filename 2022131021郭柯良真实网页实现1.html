Astronomical anecdote

  • The first person to observe space through a telescope was Galileo, 400 years ago.
  • Space is bendable. In the beginning, it was expanding at a measurable rate.
  • Cosmic rays are high-energy particles that travel through our solar system from deep outer space, but astronomers aren't sure where they come from.
  • A spacesuit costs about $11 million
 
 
 
  • Light (photons) travel from the surface of the Sun to Earth in 8 minutes and 22 seconds, while it takes 100,000 years to get from the core of the Sun to Earth.
  • It takes 200 million years for the Sun to orbit the Milky Way.
  • If astronauts don't exercise for at least two hours a day, they will lose 1% of their muscle mass every month in space.
  • There could be as many as 20 trillion galaxies in the universe.
